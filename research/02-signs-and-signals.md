# Ontario G1 Knowledge Test — Traffic Signs & Signals Reference

> **Source:** Ontario MTO Official Driver's Handbook (ontario.ca). Neutral factual survey for G1 knowledge test preparation. All sections aligned to the 20-question signs-and-signals portion of the test.

---

## 1. Sign Shapes & Colors — Master Reference

Every sign's shape and color carries a legal meaning independent of its text or symbol.

| Shape | Color | Category | Key Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Octagon** (8 sides) | Red + white letters | Regulatory — mandatory stop | Stop sign |
| **Equilateral triangle** (point down) | White + red border | Regulatory — yield | Yield sign |
| **Diamond** | Yellow + black | Warning — hazard ahead | Curve, slippery when wet |
| **Diamond** | Orange + black | Temporary/construction | Flagger ahead, lane closed |
| **Pentagon** (5-sided, point up) | Fluorescent yellow-green + black | School zone warning | School zone |
| **Rectangle / Square** (portrait) | White + black | Regulatory | Speed limit, turn prohibition |
| **Rectangle / Square** | Green + white | Information / destination | Highway exit, distance to city |
| **Rectangle / Square** | Blue + white | Services | Gas, food, hospital, info |
| **Rectangle / Square** | Brown + white | Tourist attractions / parks | Provincial park, historic site |
| **Rectangle / Square** | Orange + black | Temporary / construction info | Detour, road work ahead |
| **Pennant** (horizontal triangle) | Yellow | No-passing zone warning | Posted on left side of road |
| **Circle** | Yellow + black | Railway crossing advance warning | Advance warning circle sign |
| **X-shape (crossbuck)** | White + red outline | Railway crossing | At the crossing itself |
| **Orange triangle** + red border | Orange + red | Slow-moving vehicle | Vehicle ≤40 km/h |

**Memory cues:**
- Red = stop or prohibition
- Yellow = caution / warning
- Orange = construction / temporary
- Green = go / information / destination
- Blue = services available nearby
- Brown = recreational or cultural attractions
- White = regulatory (rules you must follow)
- Fluorescent yellow-green = school zones / pedestrian crossings

**Sources:** [ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/signs](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/signs)

## 2. Regulatory Signs

Regulatory signs have the force of law. Disobeying them is an offence under the Highway Traffic Act.

### General Regulatory Sign Look
- Usually **rectangular or square**, white or black background, black/white/coloured letters
- **Green circle** = activity shown is **permitted or required**
- **Red circle with diagonal line** = activity shown is **prohibited**

### Core Regulatory Signs

| Sign | Shape + Color | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Stop** | Red octagon, white letters "STOP" | Come to a complete stop at the stop line, crosswalk, or edge of intersection; proceed only when safe |
| **Yield** | White downward triangle, red border | Slow down or stop; give right-of-way to traffic and pedestrians already in or approaching the intersection |
| **Do Not Enter** | Red square, white horizontal bar | You may not enter this road in the direction you are travelling (typically wrong-way on one-way) |
| **One Way** | Black rectangle, white arrow + "ONE WAY" | Traffic flows in one direction only; arrow shows that direction |
| **No U-Turn** | White square, red circle/slash over U-turn symbol | U-turns prohibited at this location |
| **No Left Turn** | White square, red circle/slash over left-turn arrow | Left turns prohibited |
| **No Right Turn** | White square, red circle/slash over right-turn arrow | Right turns prohibited |
| **Speed Limit** | White rectangle, black numerals | Maximum legal speed in km/h under ideal conditions |
| **Minimum Speed** | White rectangle, black numerals with downward arrow | You must not drive slower than this speed |
| **Keep Right** | White rectangle, arrow pointing right | Keep to the right of a median or island |
| **No Passing** | White rectangle, "NO PASSING" or symbol | Passing prohibited on this stretch |
| **HOV Lane** | White rectangle, diamond symbol + occupancy requirement | High-Occupancy Vehicle lane; minimum number of occupants required (e.g., 2+, 3+) |
| **Accessible Parking** | Blue square, wheelchair symbol | Reserved for vehicles with accessible parking permit |
| **School Zone** | Pentagon (5-sided), fluorescent yellow-green | School zone ahead; reduced speed limit in effect during school hours |
| **Community Safety Zone** | Rectangular, orange-red border | Zone with doubled fines; often near schools, parks, hospitals |
| **No Parking** | White/red, "NO PARKING" or P with slash | Stopping to wait or leave vehicle prohibited |
| **No Stopping** | White/red, "NO STOPPING" | Vehicle must not stop for any reason |
| **School Bus Stop Zone** | Yellow, school bus symbol | Designated bus stop for loading/unloading children |
| **Bicycle Route** | White rectangle, bicycle symbol | Route designated for cyclists; share road |
| **Turn-Only Lane** | White rectangle, arrow | Lane may be used only for the direction shown |

**Sources:** [ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/signs](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/signs)

## 3. Warning Signs

Warning signs alert drivers to potentially dangerous conditions or changes in road character ahead. They are **advisory**, not commands — but drivers must adjust speed and behaviour accordingly.

### General Look
- **Diamond-shaped**, **yellow background**, **black letters or symbols**
- Posted in advance of the hazard

### Road Geometry & Surface Warnings

| Sign | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Curve Ahead** | Road curves ahead; reduce speed |
| **Sharp Turn / Reverse Curve** | Tight bend or S-curve ahead |
| **Winding Road** | Multiple curves ahead |
| **Steep Hill (Down)** | Steep downgrade ahead; test brakes, use lower gear |
| **Steep Hill (Up)** | Steep upgrade ahead |
| **Bump / Dip** | Rough road surface or dip in road ahead |
| **Slippery When Wet** | Road surface becomes very slippery in wet weather; reduce speed when raining |
| **Narrow Bridge** | Bridge is narrower than the road; proceed with caution |
| **Pavement Narrows** | Road lanes reduce ahead; merge required |
| **Uneven Pavement / Rough Road** | Surface is rough or irregular |

### Intersection & Traffic Warnings

| Sign | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Intersection Ahead** | Cross-road, T-junction, or Y-junction ahead |
| **Hidden Intersection** | Side road crosses ahead but is not visible due to limited sight lines |
| **Roundabout Ahead** | Traffic circle ahead; yield to vehicles already in the circle |
| **Traffic Signals Ahead** | Traffic lights coming up; be prepared to stop |
| **Stop Sign Ahead** | A stop sign is ahead; prepare to stop |

### Divided Highway Signs

| Sign | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Divided Highway Begins** | Road splits into two separate roadways with a median; keep right of divider |
| **Divided Highway Ends** | Two-way traffic resumes on one roadway; prepare for oncoming traffic |

### Lane Warning Signs

| Sign | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Lane Reduction / Merge** | Number of lanes decreases ahead; merge early |
| **Right (or Left) Lane Ends** | Specific lane ends; move to adjacent lane |

### Pedestrian & Cyclist Warnings

| Sign | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pedestrian Crossover Ahead** | Pedestrian crossover (PXO) is ahead; prepare to stop and yield |
| **School Crossing Ahead** | School crossing area ahead; watch for children and crossing guards |
| **Bicycle Crossing** | Cyclists may cross the road ahead |

### Animal Warnings

| Sign | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Deer Crossing** | Deer or wildlife frequently cross this area; watch for animals on road |
| **Cattle Crossing** | Livestock may be on or crossing the road |

### Railway Crossing Warnings

| Sign | Shape + Color | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Advance Railway Warning** | **Yellow circle**, black X and "RR" | Railway crossing ahead; slow down, look both ways, prepare to stop |
| **Railway Crossbuck** | **X-shaped**, white + red outline | You are at the railway crossing; yield to trains; stop at least 5 metres from nearest rail if train is coming |
| **Flashing Red Lights at Crossing** | Red flashing lights + bell | A train is approaching; stop and wait; do not proceed until lights stop and train has fully passed |
| **Gates at Crossing** | Lowered barrier arm | Do not go around the gate; wait until it rises |

**Memory cue — Advance warning circle:** Only **circular** warning sign you'll encounter. The circle = railway.

**Sources:** [ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/signs](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/signs)

## 4. Construction / Temporary Condition Signs

### General Look
- **Diamond-shaped** (same as warning), **orange background**, **black letters or symbols**
- Mark work zones, detours, lane shifts, and the presence of workers

### Common Construction Signs

| Sign | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Road Work Ahead** | Construction zone starts ahead; reduce speed |
| **Flagger / Traffic Control Person Ahead** | A person is directing traffic; obey their signals; they have the same authority as a traffic signal |
| **Lane Shift** | Your lane moves laterally ahead; follow the new path |
| **Survey Crew** | Survey workers may be near or on the road |
| **Pilot Vehicle / Follow Pilot** | Follow the lead vehicle through the construction area |
| **Milled / Grooved Pavement** | Road surface has been milled (rough); reduced traction, especially for motorcycles |
| **Detour Ahead** | Your regular route is closed; follow detour signs |
| **Lane Closed Ahead** | One or more lanes close ahead; merge early |
| **Reduced Speed Ahead (in construction)** | Speed limit is lower within the work zone |
| **Construction Zone Speed Enforcement** | Automated speed cameras or enhanced enforcement in effect; fines double in construction zones |
| **End Construction** | Work zone ends; regular speed limits resume |

**Key rules in construction zones:**
- Obey the posted reduced speed limit — fines are doubled for speeding in construction zones
- A flagger's hand signals and signs have the same legal force as a traffic signal
- Be prepared to stop suddenly

**Sources:** [ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/signs](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/signs)

## 6. Pavement Markings

### Line Color Rule — The Fundamental Principle

| Color | Separates |
|---|---|
| **Yellow** | Traffic travelling in **opposite** directions |
| **White** | Traffic travelling in the **same** direction |

### Yellow Line Combinations (Opposing Traffic)

| Marking | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Single broken yellow** | You may pass if safe; broken = passing permitted |
| **Single solid yellow** | Do not pass; solid on your left = unsafe to pass |
| **Double solid yellow** | No passing in **either** direction |
| **Solid + broken yellow (pair)** | Passing permitted only from the side with the broken line |

### White Line Combinations (Same Direction)

| Marking | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Single broken white** | Lane change or passing permitted when safe |
| **Single solid white** | Lane change discouraged; do not cross except when necessary (e.g., merging) |
| **Double solid white** | Do not cross (e.g., HOV lane boundary at non-access point) |

### Special Pavement Markings

| Marking | Description | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Stop line** | Single wide white line across the lane | Stop here at red lights, stop signs, and crosswalks |
| **Crosswalk** | Two parallel white lines across the road | Pedestrian crossing zone; yield to pedestrians |
| **Pedestrian Crossover (PXO) markings** | Double white parallel lines + **X in each approaching lane** | Designated crossover; drivers and cyclists must stop and yield |
| **White directional arrows** | Painted arrows in lane | You may only proceed in the direction shown |
| **Continuity lines** | Wider, closer-spaced broken white lines | Your lane is ending or exiting; change lanes to continue |
| **HOV diamond** | White diamond painted on pavement | High-Occupancy Vehicle lane; minimum occupancy required (posted on signs) |
| **Bike box** | Painted box with white bicycle symbol at intersection | Bicycle waiting area at signal; stop **behind** the box, not in it |
| **Bike lane** | Solid white line + bicycle symbol | Reserved for cyclists; drivers must not use except to cross when turning |
| **Two-way left turn lane (TWLTL)** | Centre lane marked with double yellow on each side + left-turn arrows in both directions | Shared left-turn lane; used only to prepare for and complete a left turn from either direction |
| **Yellow and black chevrons / markings** | Diagonal yellow/black stripes | Guide traffic around fixed obstacles (bridge piers, barriers) |

**Sources:** [ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/pavement-markings](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/pavement-markings)

## 7. Traffic Signals

### Steady Lights

| Signal | Meaning | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Steady Green circle** | Go, intersection is open | Proceed if intersection is clear; yield to pedestrians and vehicles already there; yield to pedestrians when turning |
| **Steady Yellow / Amber** | Red is about to appear | Stop if you can do so safely; if you cannot stop safely, proceed with caution |
| **Steady Red circle** | Stop | Stop at stop line, crosswalk, or edge of intersection; wait for green |
| **Right turn on red** | Permitted after a full stop (unless sign prohibits) | Stop completely, yield to pedestrians and cross traffic, then turn right |
| **Left turn on red** | Only from a **one-way street** onto a **one-way street**, after a full stop | Stop completely, yield, then turn left onto the one-way |

### Flashing Lights

| Signal | Meaning | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Flashing Green (Advance Green)** | Protected phase — oncoming traffic has red | You may turn left, go straight, or turn right from the proper lane; pedestrians must not cross unless pedestrian signal shows walk |
| **Flashing Red** | Treat as a stop sign | Come to a complete stop; proceed only when safe |
| **Flashing Yellow** | Caution | Slow down; proceed with care through the intersection |
| **Flashing Yellow Arrow** | Permissive left turn — yield required | You may turn left but must yield to oncoming traffic and pedestrians |

### Arrows

| Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Steady Green Arrow** | Protected movement in that direction; conflicting traffic has red |
| **Flashing Green Arrow (Advance Green Arrow)** | Protected left-turn phase; oncoming traffic faces red; safe to turn left |
| **Yellow Arrow** | Arrow phase is ending; stop if you can safely, otherwise complete the turn with caution |
| **Red Arrow** | Do not move in the direction of the arrow |

### Fully Protected vs. Permissive Left Turns

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Fully Protected** | Left-turn signal controls the left-turn lane independently; you turn left only on the green arrow; no oncoming traffic during this phase |
| **Permissive (simultaneous)** | Both you and oncoming traffic may turn left at the same time on green arrows facing each other; or you turn left on a circular green by yielding to oncoming traffic |

### Special Signals

| Signal | Appearance | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Transit Priority Signal** | White **vertical bar** on dark background | Public transit vehicle may proceed; all conflicting traffic must stop and yield |
| **Lane Control — Green Arrow (overhead)** | Green downward arrow overhead | This lane is open; you may use it |
| **Lane Control — Red X (overhead)** | Red X overhead | This lane is **closed**; do not use it |
| **Lane Control — Yellow X (overhead)** | Flashing or steady yellow X overhead | Lane is closing; move out of this lane as soon as safe |

### Non-Functional Traffic Lights

If traffic signals are not working (power outage):
- Treat the intersection as an **all-way stop**
- Yield to vehicles already in the intersection
- Yield to vehicles entering from your right
- Proceed with caution

### Traffic Beacons

| Beacon | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Flashing Red beacon** | Stop completely; proceed only when safe (same as stop sign) |
| **Flashing Yellow beacon** | Slow down; proceed with caution |

## 8. Pedestrian Crossovers (PXO) vs. School Crossings vs. Intersection Crosswalks

### Definitions

| Type | What It Is |
|---|---|
| **Intersection crosswalk** | Pedestrian crossing at a signalized or stop-controlled intersection; usually marked by two white parallel lines |
| **Pedestrian Crossover (PXO)** | Mid-block or intersection crossing with specific signs, pavement markings, and often overhead flashing yellow lights; pedestrians activate with a push button |
| **School crossing** | Designated crossing near a school, controlled by a school crossing guard or a school-crossing stop sign |

### Driver Rules at Each Type

#### Intersection Crosswalk (with traffic signal)
- Yield to pedestrians on a **green light** when turning
- Do **not** proceed if a pedestrian is in the crosswalk in your path
- At a **flashing green** or **advance green arrow**: pedestrians must **not** cross; drivers do not need to yield to pedestrians starting to cross

#### Pedestrian Crossover (PXO)
- Drivers and cyclists **must stop and yield the entire width of the roadway** to any pedestrian or person using a wheelchair in the crossover
- Yield until the pedestrian has **fully crossed** and is safely on the sidewalk
- **Do not pass any vehicle within 30 metres of a pedestrian crossover** (this applies to vehicles stopped at or approaching the crossover)
- Pedestrians may activate **overhead yellow flashing lights** before entering to warn drivers
- **Penalty for failing to yield:** Up to $1,000 fine + **4 demerit points**; fines double in Community Safety Zones

#### School Crossing (with crossing guard)
- When a crossing guard displays a **red and white stop sign**, you **must stop** before reaching the crossing
- You must **remain stopped** until all pedestrians AND the crossing guard have cleared the entire roadway
- **Penalty:** Substantial fine + **3 demerit points**

### The 30-Metre Rule (PXO-Specific)
> You must **not overtake or pass** any vehicle within **30 metres** of a pedestrian crossover on a highway with a speed limit of 60 km/h or less.

This rule is enforced even if there are no pedestrians present — it is a standing prohibition.

## 9. School Bus Signals

### Signal Sequence

School buses use a **two-stage signal system** to warn drivers of an upcoming stop.

| Phase | Lights | Meaning for Other Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| **Approaching stop** | **Amber (yellow) lights flashing** | Bus is slowing to stop; slow down and **prepare to stop** |
| **Stopped, loading/unloading** | **Red lights flashing alternately** + **stop arm extended** | Children are boarding or exiting; **you must stop** |

### When You Must Stop

- **From behind the bus:** Stop at least **20 metres** away
- **From the front (oncoming):** Stop at a safe distance to allow children to cross safely
- **Both directions** must stop on an **undivided road** (no raised median or barrier)
- **Divided road exception:** Only vehicles **behind** the bus (same direction) must stop; oncoming vehicles on the other side of a raised median do not need to stop

### When You May Proceed

You may not proceed until:
1. The bus begins to move, **or**
2. The red lights have **stopped flashing** and the stop arm has been retracted

### Penalties

| Offence | Fine | Demerit Points |
|---|---|---|
| First offence — failing to stop for school bus | **$400 – $2,000** | **6 demerit points** |
| Subsequent offences | Higher fines, possible licence suspension | 6 demerit points |

> 6 demerit points is the maximum single-offence demerit penalty for most violations. For G1/G2 drivers, 6 demerit points triggers a warning letter; 9 points triggers a licence suspension.

## 10. Emergency Vehicle Lights & Sirens

### When an Emergency Vehicle Approaches (Lights + Siren Active)

Follow this sequence:
1. **Slow down**
2. **Signal**
3. **Move to the right side of the road**, clear of any intersection
4. **Stop**

After the vehicle passes: check for safety, signal, and re-enter traffic.

**Special cases:**
- **Intersection:** Do not turn left; go through the intersection first, then pull right and stop
- **One-way street:** If right is not accessible, you may move to the left

### Move Over Law — When Passing a Stopped Emergency Vehicle

When an emergency vehicle or tow truck is **stopped on the roadside** with lights flashing in the **same direction** as your travel:
- **Reduce speed** and proceed with caution
- **Move into a lane away** from the stopped vehicle if you can do so safely (on a road with 2+ lanes in your direction)

| Vehicle | Light Color |
|---|---|
| Police car | **Red and blue** alternating (or red alone) |
| Ambulance / fire truck | **Red** (flashing) |
| Tow truck (on highway shoulder) | **Amber (yellow)** flashing |

> **Note:** A tow truck with amber lights stopped on the highway is covered by the same "slow down and move over" rules as emergency vehicles.

**Prohibition:** It is illegal to follow within **150 metres** of a fire vehicle or ambulance responding to a call.

### Penalties — Failing to Yield / Move Over

| Offence | Fine | Demerit Points | Other |
|---|---|---|---|
| First offence | $400 – $2,000 | 3 | Possible licence suspension up to 2 years |
| Second offence (within 5 years) | $1,000 – $4,000 | 3 | Up to 6 months jail; suspension up to 2 years |

## 11. Lane Control Signals (Overhead)

Lane control signals are mounted **above individual lanes**, typically on highways, bridges, or reversible-lane roads. They control which lanes are open or closed.

### Signal Meanings

| Signal | Appearance | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Green Arrow (downward)** | Green downward-pointing arrow | Lane is **open**; you may use this lane |
| **Red X** | Red X | Lane is **closed**; you must not drive in this lane |
| **Yellow X** | Flashing or steady yellow X | Lane is **closing**; move out of this lane as soon as it is safe to do so |

### Where Used
- Reversible lanes (e.g., peak-hour traffic management)
- Bridge lanes or tunnel approaches
- High-occupancy vehicle lane entry/exit points
- Contraflow lanes during construction

### Key Rule
> A **red X** overhead has the same legal effect as a red traffic light for that lane. You must not occupy a lane with a red X displayed above it.
