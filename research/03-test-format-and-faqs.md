# Ontario G1 Knowledge Test — Format & Commonly Tested Facts

> Neutral survey. Sources cross-referenced from drivetest.ca, ontario.ca, g1.ca, learnontario.ca, trubicars.ca, thinkinsure.ca, mydl.ca, g1practicetest.ca, and others. Last verified May 2026.

---

## 1. Logistics

### Where to Take It
- Any **DriveTest Centre** across Ontario (walk-in accepted at most locations, no appointment required)
- Exception: verbal/audio accommodation requires a scheduled appointment
- Source: [drivetest.ca](https://drivetest.ca/) | [trubicars.ca](https://www.trubicars.ca/g1-licence/)

### 2026 Fee
| What you pay | Amount (CAD) |
|---|---|
| G1 package (knowledge test + first G2 road test + 5-year licence) | **$159.75** |
| Knowledge test retake (if you fail) | **~$16** |
| G2 road test retake | $53.75 |
| G road test | $91.25 |

- The $159.75 package covers three things in one: the written exam, one future G2 road-test attempt, and the 5-year licence fee.
- Sources: [learnontario.ca](https://learnontario.ca/g1-to-g-licence-ontario-complete-timeline-costs-2025/) | [g1.ca — cost guide](https://www.g1.ca/driving-tips/how-much-is-g1-test-ontario/)

### Age Requirement
- Must be **at least 16 years old**

### What to Bring — ID Requirements
- Original documents (no photocopies) proving **legal name** + **complete date of birth** (day/month/year) + **signature**
- Acceptable primary ID:
  - Canadian passport (or foreign passport)
  - Canadian birth certificate
  - Canadian Citizenship Card with photo
  - Permanent Resident Card
  - Student Authorization or Employment Authorization
- If name on documents differs (e.g., maiden name), bring a supporting document (e.g., marriage certificate)
- Bring **prescription glasses or contact lenses** if you need them — eye exam is part of the process
- Bring **payment** (debit or credit card accepted)
- Sources: [ontario.ca — getting your licence](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/getting-your-drivers-licence) | [thinkinsure.ca](https://www.thinkinsure.ca/insurance-help-centre/g1-test-ontario.html)

### Eye Test
- A vision screening is mandatory at the DriveTest centre before the knowledge test
- Bring corrective lenses if you wear them

### Online Booking
- Walk-in is accepted at most centres — no advance booking needed
- If you require a verbal/audio accommodation, book in advance

### If You Fail — Retake Policy
- Retake fee: ~$16 per attempt
- No mandatory waiting period specified between retakes
- You can retake as many times as needed until you pass
- The G1 package ($159.75) remains valid for your eventual G2 road test

## 2. Test Format

### Structure
| | |
|---|---|
| **Total questions** | 40 |
| **Section A — Road Signs** | 20 questions |
| **Section B — Rules of the Road** | 20 questions |
| **Question format** | Multiple choice, 4 options |
| **Passing score** | **16/20 (80%) in EACH section** — both must be passed independently |
| **Time limit** | **None** — the test is untimed |
| **Duration in practice** | ~20–30 minutes |
| **Delivery** | Touchscreen computer at most centres; paper/verbal available as accommodations |
| **Languages available** | 27 languages (Arabic, French, German, Greek, Hindi, Italian, Korean, Polish, Portuguese, Punjabi, Spanish, Cree, and others) |
| **Results** | Given same day |

- Failing one section while passing the other = fail overall; retake required
- Questions are drawn from the **official Ontario Driver's Handbook** (MTO)
- Sources: [trubicars.ca](https://www.trubicars.ca/g1-practice-tests/) | [g1.ca](https://www.g1.ca/g1-test/) | [tests.ca](https://tests.ca/ontario/ontario-g1-test-practice/)

## 3. After Passing — What Happens Next

### G1 Issued Same Day
- You receive a **Class G1 licence** immediately after passing
- Valid for **5 years**

### G1 Restrictions (what you can and can't do)
| Restriction | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Supervising driver required** | Must have a fully licensed driver (4+ years) in the front passenger seat at all times |
| **Driving alone** | Not permitted |
| **Driving hours** | **5:00 am to midnight only** — prohibited midnight to 5 am |
| **BAC (blood alcohol)** | **Zero (0.00%)** — absolute zero tolerance |
| **Highway 400-series / high-speed expressways** | **Prohibited** — includes Gardiner, DVP, QEW, all 400-series |
| **Exception for expressways** | Only permitted if accompanied by a licensed driving instructor |
| **Passengers** | No more passengers than there are working seatbelts; all must be buckled |

- Sources: [progressivedriving.ca G1 conditions](https://progressivedriving.ca/assets/uploads/G1_CONDITIONS_2019.pdf) | [dominiondrivingschool.com](https://dominiondrivingschool.com/g1-license-restrictions-ontario/) | [thinkinsure.ca](https://www.thinkinsure.ca/insurance-help-centre/ontario-graduated-g-g1-g2-licence-system-guide.html)

### Waiting Period for G2 Road Test
| Path | Wait time |
|---|---|
| Standard | **12 months** from G1 issue date |
| With approved Beginner Driver Education (BDE) | **8 months** (saves 4 months) |

### BDE — Beginner Driver Education
- Must be a ministry-approved course
- Typically: 20 hours theory + 10 hours flexible/homework + 10 hours in-car
- Cost: ~$600–$900+ (varies by location)
- Benefit: reduces G1-to-G2 wait from 12 → 8 months

### Full G Licence Timeline
| Stage | Standard | With BDE |
|---|---|---|
| G1 → G2 road test wait | 12 months | 8 months |
| G2 → G road test wait | 12 months | 12 months (no shortcut) |
| **Total minimum** | **24 months** | **20 months** |

- Sources: [learnontario.ca](https://learnontario.ca/g1-to-g-licence-ontario-complete-timeline-costs-2025/) | [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/page/get-g-drivers-licence-new-drivers)

## 4. Top Commonly Tested Specific Facts (Q&A Format)

### Speed Limits

**Q: What is the default speed limit in cities, towns, and villages where no limit is posted?**
A: **50 km/h**

**Q: What is the default speed limit in rural/unposted areas outside populated areas?**
A: **80 km/h**

**Q: What governs the actual safe speed regardless of posted limits?**
A: You must always drive at a speed that allows you to **stop safely** — the maximum posted limit is just that: a maximum. Conditions (weather, visibility, traffic) may require slower speeds.

---

### Following Distance

**Q: What is the minimum safe following distance behind the vehicle ahead?**
A: **At least 2 seconds** (two-second rule)

**Q: How do you measure the 2-second following distance?**
A: Pick a fixed marker (road sign, pole). When the rear of the vehicle ahead passes it, count "one-thousand-and-one, one-thousand-and-two." If you reach the marker before finishing, you're too close.

**Q: When should you increase your following distance beyond 2 seconds?**
A: In bad weather, poor visibility, when following motorcycles or large trucks, when carrying heavy loads, or on slippery roads. On snowy/icy roads, use at least 4 seconds (double the normal).

**Q: What following distance must commercial vehicles maintain on highways outside urban areas?**
A: At least **120 metres (400 feet)**

---

### Signalling

**Q: How far before a turn must you signal in a 50 km/h zone?**
A: At least **30 metres** before the turn

**Q: How far before a turn must you signal on a highway?**
A: At least **150 metres** before the turn

**Q: When else must you signal?**
A: Before slowing down, stopping, changing lanes, leaving a parked position, or pulling off the road

---

### School Bus Rules

**Q: How far back must you stop behind a stopped school bus with flashing red lights?**
A: At least **20 metres** from the bus

**Q: Must you stop when approaching a stopped school bus from the opposite direction on an undivided road?**
A: **Yes** — you must stop a safe distance away for children to cross

**Q: Is there an exception for divided roads?**
A: Yes — on roads divided by a **median barrier** (raised or otherwise), oncoming traffic does NOT have to stop

**Q: What do amber (yellow) flashing lights on a school bus mean?**
A: The bus is preparing to stop. **Slow down and prepare to stop** — do not pass

**Q: What do red flashing lights + stop arm on a school bus mean?**
A: Bus is stopped, children are loading/unloading. **You must stop completely** — from either direction (unless divided road with median)

**Q: When can you proceed after a school bus stop?**
A: Only when the red lights have stopped flashing AND the stop arm is no longer extended (bus moves or lights stop)

**Q: Does this rule apply only during school hours?**
A: **No** — it applies any time, on any road, whenever the lights are activated

**Q: Fine for illegally passing a stopped school bus?**
A: **$400–$2,000** (first conviction) + **6 demerit points**

---

### Pedestrian Crossovers vs. Crosswalks

**Q: What is a pedestrian crossover?**
A: A designated crossing (marked by two double white parallel lines with an X in each lane) where pedestrians have absolute right-of-way; typically has overhead lights activated by a button

**Q: What must drivers do at a pedestrian crossover?**
A: **Yield completely** — stop and wait until the pedestrian has crossed the entire road and is safely on the sidewalk

**Q: Can you pass a vehicle stopped at a pedestrian crossover?**
A: **No** — never pass within 30 metres of a pedestrian crossover

**Q: Fine for failing to yield at a pedestrian crossover?**
A: Up to **$500** + **4 demerit points**

**Q: At a marked crosswalk at an intersection with traffic lights, who has right-of-way?**
A: Pedestrians who are already crossing when you get the green — you must wait for them before turning

---

### Intersections & Right-of-Way

**Q: At a 4-way stop, vehicles arrive at the same time. Who goes first?**
A: The driver to the **right** has the right-of-way ("yield to the right")

**Q: At a 4-way stop, one vehicle arrives first. Who goes first?**
A: The vehicle that **arrived first** goes first

**Q: At an uncontrolled intersection (no signs or lights), who yields?**
A: The driver on the **left** yields to the driver on the **right**

**Q: What must you do when entering a highway from a private road or driveway?**
A: **Yield to all vehicles** approaching on the highway

**Q: At a roundabout, who yields?**
A: Vehicles entering the roundabout must **yield to traffic already inside** — circulating traffic has right-of-way

---

### Traffic Lights

**Q: What does a green light mean?**
A: You may go — but must yield to pedestrians and vehicles already in the intersection

**Q: What does a yellow/amber light mean?**
A: Stop if you can do so safely. If stopping would be dangerous (you're too close), proceed with caution.

**Q: What does a red light mean?**
A: Full stop. Right turn on red is permitted after stopping and checking, unless a sign says "No Right Turn on Red."

**Q: When is a left turn on red permitted?**
A: When turning **from a one-way street onto another one-way street** — stop first, confirm clear, then turn

**Q: What does a flashing green light (advanced green) mean?**
A: You have the advanced right-of-way — you may turn left, go straight, or turn right; oncoming traffic faces red

**Q: What does a flashing red light mean?**
A: Treat as a **stop sign** — come to a complete stop, proceed when safe

**Q: What does a flashing yellow light mean?**
A: **Proceed with caution** — slow down and be alert

**Q: If traffic lights are completely out (power failure), how do you treat the intersection?**
A: As an **all-way stop** — all drivers stop and yield to vehicles already in the intersection or to the right

---

### Passing Rules

**Q: What does a broken yellow centre line on your side mean?**
A: **Passing is permitted** if the way is clear and it's safe to do so

**Q: What does a solid yellow centre line on your side mean?**
A: **No passing** — it is unsafe

**Q: What does a combination of solid + broken yellow centre line mean?**
A: The driver beside the **broken line** may pass; the driver beside the **solid line** may not

**Q: When may you pass on the right?**
A: When the vehicle ahead is turning left and there is enough room on the right; or on a road with two or more lanes in the same direction

**Q: When is passing prohibited regardless of line markings?**
A: Within 30 metres of a pedestrian crossover; within 30 metres before a bridge, tunnel, or railway crossing; on a hill or curve where you can't see far enough ahead; in intersections (in most cases)

---

### U-Turns

**Q: What is your first consideration before making a U-turn?**
A: Check **traffic regulations** — is it legally permitted here? (This is the MTO's official answer from the sample test)

**Q: Where are U-turns generally prohibited?**
A: Where a sign prohibits it; on curves, hills, or anywhere visibility is limited; in intersections controlled by traffic lights (generally); near bridges, railway crossings, or within 150 metres of a bridge

---

### Stopping Distances & Skid Recovery

**Q: What is the first action to take when your vehicle goes into a skid?**
A: **Steer in the direction you want to go** (the direction of the skid is the official MTO phrasing: "steer in the direction you want to travel") — do NOT brake hard

**Q: What should you do if your vehicle starts to hydroplane?**
A: Do not brake or accelerate suddenly. Ease off the gas and **steer straight** until tires regain traction

**Q: Why is black ice particularly dangerous?**
A: Because **you cannot see it** — it's invisible on the road surface

---

### Pavement Markings

**Q: What color lines separate traffic moving in opposite directions?**
A: **Yellow** lines

**Q: What color lines separate traffic moving in the same direction?**
A: **White** lines

**Q: What does a single broken white line between lanes mean?**
A: You may change lanes if safe

**Q: What does a solid white line between lanes mean?**
A: You should not change lanes (though it's not always illegal — it warns against it)

**Q: What do white continuity/exit lines (wider, closer-spaced broken lines) on the right mean?**
A: Your lane is ending or exiting — you must change lanes to continue

**Q: What do white arrows painted in a lane mean?**
A: You may only travel in the direction shown

---

### Parking Rules

**Q: Minimum distance to park from a fire hydrant?**
A: **3 metres**

**Q: Minimum distance to park from an uncontrolled intersection?**
A: **9 metres**

**Q: Minimum distance to park from a traffic-light-controlled intersection?**
A: **15 metres**

**Q: Can you park on a bridge or within 100 metres of one?**
A: No — prohibited

---

### Blood Alcohol & Impairment

**Q: What is the BAC limit for a G1 driver?**
A: **Zero (0.00%)** — absolute zero tolerance; any detectable alcohol = violation

**Q: What is the BAC limit for a fully licensed (G) driver who is 22+?**
A: The legal limit in Ontario is **0.08%**, but drivers showing 0.05%–0.079% face immediate 3-day suspension + $250 penalty (warn range)

**Q: What is the BAC limit for G2 drivers?**
A: Also **zero (0.00%)** — G2 drivers are novice drivers under zero-tolerance rules

---

### Demerit Points — Key Ones Tested

| Offence | Demerit Points |
|---|---|
| Passing a stopped school bus | 6 |
| Careless driving | 6 |
| Failing to remain at scene of collision | 7 |
| Speeding 30–49 km/h over limit | 4 |
| Speeding 50+ km/h over limit | 6 |
| Failing to yield to pedestrian at crossover | 4 |
| Improper passing | 4 |
| Following too closely | 4 |
| Disobeying stop sign | 3 |
| Failing to yield right-of-way | 3 |
| Failing to stop for school crossing guard | 3 |

**Q: At how many demerit points does a fully licensed driver face a warning letter?**
A: **9 points** — warning letter; at 15 points, licence is suspended

**Q: At how many demerit points does a G1/G2 driver face suspension?**
A: **9 points** (lower threshold for novice drivers — suspension at 9)

---

### Emergency Vehicles

**Q: What must you do when an emergency vehicle with lights/sirens is approaching?**
A: Pull to the right side of the road and **stop** until it passes — emergency vehicles always have right-of-way

**Q: What is the "Move Over" law?**
A: When passing a stopped emergency vehicle, tow truck, or road maintenance vehicle with flashing lights on the shoulder, you must move over one lane if safe; if not safe, slow down significantly

---

### Tire Rules

**Q: When must tires be replaced in Ontario (minimum tread depth)?**
A: When tread is **less than 1.5 mm** deep (or when tread-wear indicators touch the road surface)

**Q: Is Ontario required to use winter tires?**
A: No — winter tires are not legally mandatory in Ontario (unlike Quebec). However, most insurers offer a discount for using them.

---

### Highway / Freeway Driving

**Q: What should you do if you miss your exit on a highway?**
A: Continue to the next exit — do NOT back up or stop on the highway

**Q: What do you do if your vehicle breaks down on a highway?**
A: Pull completely off the road if possible, turn on hazard lights, stay in the vehicle or well away from traffic

**Q: At what speed must you travel on the acceleration lane of a highway?**
A: Match the speed of highway traffic before merging

---

### Miscellaneous Tested Facts

**Q: May you lend your driver's licence to another person?**
A: **No — never permitted** (MTO sample test official question)

**Q: Why is driving at night at maximum speed more dangerous than daytime?**
A: Because **you cannot see as far ahead** at night as your headlights illuminate less than your stopping distance at speed

**Q: What is the minimum headlight visibility distance (white light ahead)?**
A: Headlights must illuminate at least **150 metres** ahead

**Q: How often should you check your mirrors while driving?**
A: Every **5 seconds** (glance to mirrors every 5 seconds)

**Q: When must you use headlights?**
A: Between sunset and sunrise, and any time visibility is less than 150 metres (e.g., fog, heavy rain, snow)

**Q: What must you do before entering an intersection on a green light?**
A: Still yield to pedestrians and vehicles already in the intersection

**Q: When approaching a railway crossing, when must you stop?**
A: When a train is approaching, gates are down, or crossing signals are flashing — stop at least **5 metres** from the nearest rail

**Q: Do school buses stop at railway crossings?**
A: **Yes** — always, regardless of whether a train is visible

**Q: If a police officer's directions conflict with a traffic signal, which do you obey?**
A: **The police officer** — officers override signals and signs

**Q: What is the rule for wheels when parking uphill with a curb?**
A: Turn wheels **left** (away from curb) — if brakes fail, car rolls into curb

**Q: What is the rule for wheels when parking downhill with a curb?**
A: Turn wheels **right** (toward curb)

**Q: What is the rule for parking on a hill with no curb?**
A: Turn wheels to the **right** (toward the edge of the road) in both directions

## 5. Common Trick / Confusable Questions

### 4-Way Stop Tie-Breakers
- **Simultaneous arrival**: Yield to the driver on your **right** (not the first to creep forward)
- **One arrives first**: That driver goes first regardless of direction
- Common mistake: assuming the driver going straight always has priority — they don't if they arrived after someone else

### Solid vs. Broken Yellow Lines
- **Broken on your side** → you CAN pass (if safe)
- **Solid on your side** → you CANNOT pass
- **Double solid** → no passing either direction
- The trick: the test shows a diagram and asks about the driver beside the solid line vs. beside the broken line — always refer to YOUR side

### Pedestrian Crossover vs. Crosswalk (Intersection)
- **Crossover** (mid-block, with overhead lights): must stop and wait for pedestrian to clear the ENTIRE road + sidewalk
- **Crosswalk at intersection**: must yield, but the light sequence also plays a role — green for you means you can turn after yielding to crossing pedestrians
- Common mix-up: thinking crossover and crosswalk rules are identical — the crossover has stricter stop-and-wait rules

### When You Can Pass on the Right
- Vehicle ahead is **turning left** and there is a paved lane to the right
- Road has **two or more marked lanes** going the same direction
- NOT permitted: on a shoulder, gravel, or by leaving the road surface

### U-Turn Legality — The Test's Trick
- The official MTO answer: first check **traffic regulations** (signs) — this is the priority, before checking for trees or radius
- U-turns are generally prohibited at controlled intersections in many municipalities

### Roundabout — Yielding Left
- Counter-intuitive: you might expect to yield right (like at a 4-way stop), but in a roundabout you yield to vehicles **already circulating inside** (traffic from your left)
- You enter the roundabout from the right, so circulating traffic comes from your left

### Flashing Yellow School Bus Lights vs. Red
- **Amber/yellow flashing** = bus slowing to stop → slow down, be ready to stop, but you have not yet reached the legal obligation to stop
- **Red flashing + stop arm** = full stop required immediately
- Common mistake: thinking amber lights require an immediate stop — they don't legally, but you must be prepared

### Skid Recovery — "Direction of the Skid" vs. "Direction You Want to Go"
- MTO official phrasing: "steer in the direction you want to go"
- This is the same thing — if the rear slides right, steer right to recover
- Common wrong answer: "steer opposite to the skid" — that's wrong; you steer WITH the direction you want to travel (which is the direction of the slide)

### Snow/Ice Following Distance
- In normal conditions: 2 seconds minimum
- In rain/fog: double it (4 seconds)
- In snow/ice: at least double (4+ seconds; some sources say 4–6 seconds)
- Test may ask: "How should you adjust following distance in snowy conditions?" → increase significantly

### Hydroplaning Recovery
- Don't brake suddenly
- Don't accelerate
- Ease off the gas
- Steer straight / in the direction you want to travel
- Wait for tires to regain contact

## 6. Road Signs Section — Key Points

### Sign Shape Reference
| Shape | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Octagon (8-sided) | Stop |
| Triangle (inverted, red+white) | Yield |
| Diamond (yellow) | Warning |
| Diamond (orange) | Construction/temporary |
| Rectangle (white) | Regulatory (speed, lanes, parking) |
| Rectangle (green) | Information/directions |
| Rectangle (brown) | Recreational/cultural |
| Pentagon (5-sided, school zone) | School zone warning |
| Circle | Railway crossing advance warning |

### Color Reference
| Color | Type |
|---|---|
| Red | Prohibition / Stop / Yield |
| Yellow | Warning (permanent hazard) |
| Orange | Temporary (construction) |
| White | Regulatory |
| Green | Direction / information |
| Blue | Services (gas, food, hospital) |
| Brown | Recreational/heritage |
| Fluorescent yellow-green | Pedestrian/school zones |

### Commonly Tested Signs
- **Do Not Enter** (red circle, white bar): entry prohibited — wrong-way traffic
- **No Passing** (black arrow, two cars): no passing zone
- **Keep Right** (arrow): stay to the right of obstruction
- **Roundabout** (circular arrow): roundabout ahead
- **Pedestrian Crossover** (walking figure): crossover with yield obligation
- **Railway Crossing (X)**: watch for trains
- **Slow-Moving Vehicle** (orange triangle, retroreflective): farm/slow equipment ahead
- **Divided Highway Begins**: road splits, keep right
- **Divided Highway Ends**: road merges back, watch for oncoming traffic
- **Dead End** / **No Outlet**: road doesn't connect through

### Signs Section Study Tips
- The signs section is **largely visual recognition** — learn the shape + color + symbol
- Many wrong answers on signs include plausible-sounding but incorrect meanings
- Orange = construction — you must obey, and fines can be doubled in construction zones
- Fluorescent yellow-green = pedestrian/school — highest visibility color, newer standard
- The test will show a sign image and ask what it means — no written description

## 7. Study & Test-Taking Tips

### Preparation
1. **Read the official Ontario Driver's Handbook** cover to cover — all test questions come from it. Available free at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook)
2. **Take multiple timed practice tests** — aim for consistent 18+/20 on both sections before booking
3. The signs section is visual — flip through sign images repeatedly until recognition is automatic
4. Pay special attention to specific numbers (distances, fines, demerit points) — these are frequently tested
5. Know the G1 restrictions cold — those questions appear in the rules section

### On Test Day
- No appointment needed — arrive at any DriveTest centre during business hours
- Bring ID and payment
- Wear/bring corrective lenses if needed (eye test first)
- The test is untimed — read every question carefully
- All 4 answer choices may sound plausible — pick the most complete/specific one
- If unsure between two answers, go with the more cautious/defensive option

### Common Pitfalls
- Not meeting the 16/20 threshold in **each** section — you can't compensate a low signs score with a high rules score
- Confusing "flashing yellow" (caution) with "flashing red" (stop)
- Missing the exact distances (30 m vs 150 m for signals; 20 m for school bus)
- Forgetting that G1 BAC is **zero**, not 0.05 or 0.08
- Confusing which way to turn wheels on hills (uphill with curb = away from curb = left)
