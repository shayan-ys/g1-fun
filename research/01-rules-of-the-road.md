# Ontario G1 — Rules of the Road: Research Survey

> **Scope:** Ontario only. Facts sourced from MTO Official Driver's Handbook (ontario.ca), ontario.ca policy pages, and drivetest.ca. Cross-references from g1.ca noted where used. Numbers flagged `⚠️ CONFLICT` where sources disagree.
> **Last researched:** 2026-05-09

## 1. Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC) Limits

- **Zero tolerance (0.00 BAC):** Applies to all drivers **21 years and under**, and all holders of G1, G2, M1, or M2 licences regardless of age.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Impaired Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/impaired-driving); [MTO Driver's Handbook — Getting Your Driver's Licence](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/getting-your-drivers-licence)
- **Warn range (0.05–0.079 BAC):** Triggers immediate roadside administrative suspension for fully licensed drivers.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Impaired Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/impaired-driving)
- **Criminal threshold (0.08+ BAC):** Impaired driving is a Criminal Code offence at 0.08 or above.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Impaired Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/impaired-driving)
- **Commercial drivers:** Zero tolerance; 3-day immediate roadside suspension for any BAC detected.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Impaired Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/impaired-driving)

## 2. Speed Limits

- **Default urban (city/town/village):** 50 km/h
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving Along](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-along)
- **Default rural (outside urban areas):** 80 km/h
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving Along](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-along)
- **Highways (400-series and expressways):** Typically 100 km/h (posted; can vary — always obey posted sign)
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Speeding and Aggressive Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/speeding-and-aggressive-driving)
- **School zones and construction zones:** Have lower posted limits; must obey posted signs. The general rule: always drive at a speed that allows safe stopping.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving Along](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-along)
- **Community safety zones:** Designated areas with special risk to pedestrians; traffic offences here carry **doubled fines**.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Signs](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/signs)
- **Construction zones (workers present):** Fines for speeding are **doubled** when workers are present.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Speeding and Aggressive Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/speeding-and-aggressive-driving)

### Speeding Fine Rates (per km over limit)

| Over Limit | Fine per km |
|---|---|
| 1–19 km/h over | $3.00/km |
| 20–29 km/h over | $4.50/km |
| 30–49 km/h over | $7.00/km |
| 50+ km/h over | $9.75/km |

- Source: [ontario.ca — Speeding and Aggressive Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/speeding-and-aggressive-driving)

## 4. Stunt Driving / Street Racing

### Speed Thresholds (post-2022 MOMS Act)

- **Roads with speed limit under 80 km/h:** Stunt driving charge triggered at **40 km/h or more over** the posted limit.
- **Roads with speed limit of 80 km/h or higher:** Stunt driving charge triggered at **50 km/h or more over** the posted limit.
- **Anywhere in Ontario:** Driving **150 km/h or faster** is stunt driving regardless of posted limit.
- Note: Prior to the 2022 MOMS Act (Moving Ontarians More Safely Act), the single threshold was 50 km/h over; the 40 km/h tier on lower-speed roads was introduced in 2022.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Speeding and Aggressive Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/speeding-and-aggressive-driving); [thinkinsure.ca cross-reference](https://www.thinkinsure.ca/insurance-help-centre/stunt-driving-ontario.html)

### Immediate Roadside Penalties (At Scene)

- **Licence suspension:** 30 days (immediate)
- **Vehicle impoundment:** 14 days
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Speeding and Aggressive Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/speeding-and-aggressive-driving)

### Conviction Penalties (Court)

| Conviction | Fine | Licence Suspension |
|---|---|---|
| First | $2,000–$10,000 | 1–3 years |
| Second | $2,000–$10,000 | 3–10 years |
| Third | $2,000–$10,000 | Lifetime (reducible after 10 years) |
| Fourth+ | $2,000–$10,000 | Lifetime (non-reducible) |

- **Additional penalties:** 6 demerit points; up to 6 months in jail; mandatory driver improvement course; substantial insurance impact or uninsurability.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Speeding and Aggressive Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/speeding-and-aggressive-driving)

## 5. Right-of-Way Rules

### Uncontrolled Intersections (No Signs or Signals)
- Vehicle approaching from the **right** has the right-of-way.
- If two vehicles arrive simultaneously, the driver on the **left yields** to the driver on the right.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving Through Intersections](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-through-intersections)

### Four-Way Stops
- The **first vehicle to complete a full stop** has the right-of-way.
- If two vehicles stop simultaneously, the vehicle on the **left yields** to the vehicle on the right.
- Turns must yield to oncoming traffic going straight.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving Through Intersections](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-through-intersections)

### Left Turns
- Turning left must yield to:
  - Approaching traffic going straight
  - Pedestrians crossing in or approaching your path
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving Through Intersections](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-through-intersections)

### Right Turns
- Must yield to pedestrians in your path.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving Through Intersections](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-through-intersections)

### Entering from Private Road or Driveway
- Must yield to all vehicles on the road and pedestrians on the sidewalk.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving Through Intersections](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-through-intersections)

### Pedestrian Crossovers
- Drivers must yield and **wait for pedestrians to completely cross** — not just reach the other side of your lane.
- Cannot pass another vehicle within **30 metres** of a pedestrian crossover.
- Pedestrian crossovers are marked with overhead flashing yellow lights and pavement markings.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Stopping](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/stopping)

## 6. School Bus Rules

### Flashing Light Meanings
- **Amber lights flashing:** Bus is slowing to stop to pick up or drop off passengers. Slow down and prepare to stop.
- **Red lights flashing / stop arm extended:** Bus has stopped; children are boarding or exiting. **Must stop completely.**
  - Source: [ontario.ca — School Bus Safety](https://www.ontario.ca/page/school-bus-safety)

### Stopping Distance
- If driving **behind** the bus: stop at least **20 metres** away.
- If approaching **from the front**: stop at a safe distance to allow children to exit and cross.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — School Bus Safety](https://www.ontario.ca/page/school-bus-safety); [MTO Driver's Handbook — Stopping](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/stopping)

### Divided vs. Undivided Roads
- **Undivided road (no raised median):** Traffic in **both directions** must stop.
- **Divided road (raised median between lanes):** Only traffic on the **same side** as the bus must stop; opposite-direction traffic is exempt.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — School Bus Safety](https://www.ontario.ca/page/school-bus-safety)

### Penalties for Passing a Stopped School Bus

| Offence | Fine | Demerit Points |
|---|---|---|
| First offence | $400–$2,000 | 6 |
| Subsequent (within 5 years) | $1,000–$4,000 | 6 |
| Subsequent (severe) | Up to $4,000 + possible jail (up to 6 months) | 6 |

- Source: [ontario.ca — School Bus Safety](https://www.ontario.ca/page/school-bus-safety)

## 7. Following Distance

- **Standard conditions:** Minimum **2-second rule** — after the vehicle ahead passes a fixed point, count "one-thousand-and-one, one-thousand-and-two" before you reach the same point.
- **Adverse conditions** (rain, snow, fog, heavy load, following motorcycles or large trucks): Increase to at least **3 seconds** (handbook implies greater distance, though "3 seconds" is widely cited in MTO-aligned prep materials as the adverse-conditions guideline).
  - ⚠️ **Note:** The handbook states "extra space" in adverse conditions and describes the 2-second rule explicitly; the "3-second rule" for adverse is stated in some sections but phrased as implied rather than a hard number in the primary handbook text. All major Ontario G1 prep resources use 3 seconds for adverse conditions.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving Along](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-along)

## 8. Seatbelts

- **Requirement:** Every person in the vehicle must be buckled in their own seatbelt. One seatbelt per person — sharing is not permitted.
- **Fit:** Lap belt flat across hips; shoulder belt across centre of chest and over the shoulder. Never under the arm or behind the back.
- **Driver responsibility:** Drivers are responsible for ensuring all passengers **under 16** are properly restrained.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Getting Ready to Drive](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/getting-ready-drive)

### Penalties

- Driver not wearing seatbelt: fine + **2 demerit points**
- Driver fails to ensure passenger under 16 is buckled: fine + demerit points
- G1/G2 drivers: if any passenger lacks a working seatbelt, driver faces minimum **30-day licence suspension**
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Getting Ready to Drive](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/getting-ready-drive)

## 9. Child Car Seats

| Stage | Weight | Height | Age | Required Restraint |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Infant | Up to 9 kg (20 lbs) | — | — | **Rear-facing** child car seat |
| Toddler | 9–18 kg (20–40 lbs) | — | — | **Forward-facing** child car seat (can remain rear-facing if seat accommodates) |
| Pre-school / Primary | 18–36 kg (40–80 lbs) | Under 145 cm (4'9") | Under 8 | **Booster seat** |
| Seatbelt alone | 36 kg+ (80 lbs+) OR | 145 cm+ (4'9"+) OR | 8 years+ | Regular seatbelt |

- **Key point:** A child exits the booster-seat stage when they meet **any one** of the three criteria (age 8, 36 kg, or 145 cm) — not all three.
- **Booster effectiveness:** Booster seats provide ~60% more protection than a seatbelt alone for children.
- **Rear-facing + airbags:** Never place rear-facing infant seat in front of an active airbag.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Choosing a Child Car Seat](https://www.ontario.ca/page/choosing-child-car-seat); [MTO Driver's Handbook — Getting Ready to Drive](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/getting-ready-drive)

## 10. Distracted Driving

### What Is Illegal
- Using a handheld phone/wireless device to text or call
- Operating a handheld electronic entertainment device (tablet, gaming console)
- Viewing an unrelated display screen while driving
- Programming GPS on a cellphone (unless by voice command)
- **Permitted:** Hands-free devices using earpieces, lapel buttons, Bluetooth

### Penalties — Full Licence Holders (G, A–F)

| Conviction | Fine (out-of-court) | Max Fine (court) | Demerit Points | Suspension |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| First | $615 | $1,000 | 3 | 3 days |
| Second | $615 | $2,000 | 6 | 7 days |
| Third+ | $615 | $3,000 | 6 | 30 days |

### Penalties — Novice Drivers (G1, G2, M1, M2)
- Same fine amounts as full licence holders
- **No demerit points** — instead, longer suspensions apply:
  - First conviction: **30-day suspension**
  - Second conviction: **90-day suspension**
  - Third conviction: **Licence cancellation** and removal from the Graduated Licensing System
- Licence reinstatement fee applies to each suspension.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Distracted Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/distracted-driving)

## 11. Impaired Driving Consequences

### BAC Thresholds (recap)
- **0.00:** Required for G1/G2/M1/M2 and all drivers 21 and under
- **0.05–0.079:** "Warn range" — administrative sanctions
- **0.08+:** Criminal Code offence

### Warn Range (0.05–0.079) — Administrative Roadside Suspension

⚠️ **CONFLICT between sources:**

| Source | 1st Offence | 2nd Offence | 3rd Offence |
|---|---|---|---|
| ontario.ca/page/impaired-driving (fetched 2026-05-09) | **7 days** | **14 days** | **30 days** |
| MTO Driver's Handbook — Other Ways to Lose Your Licence | **3 days** | **7 days** | *(not specified)* |

- The ontario.ca/page/impaired-driving page noted that "some penalties for impaired driving have changed starting January 1st, 2026" — the newer 7/14/30-day schedule likely reflects the current post-2026 rules.
- Traditional pre-2026 references (including classic G1 prep materials) use 3/7/30 days.
- **Use 7/14/30 days for current accuracy; note the change.**
- **Second offence also requires:** Remedial alcohol-education program
- **Third offence also requires:** Remedial alcohol-treatment program + ignition interlock condition for 6 months
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Impaired Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/impaired-driving)

### Impairment-Level Offences (0.08+, refusal to provide sample, etc.)
- Immediate **90-day roadside licence suspension**
- **7-day vehicle impoundment** (all occurrences)
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Impaired Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/impaired-driving)

### Criminal Conviction Consequences (0.08+)
- Mandatory medical evaluation for fitness to drive
- Potential **lifetime licence suspension** (may be reduced after 10 years if criteria met)
- Mandatory education or treatment program
- **Ignition interlock device required** for minimum 6 years (set to 0.02 BAC threshold)
- Fines up to $50,000; jail time
- Impaired driving causing bodily harm: up to 14 years imprisonment
- Impaired driving causing death: life imprisonment
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Other Ways to Lose Your Licence](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/other-ways-lose-your-licence); [ontario.ca — Impaired Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/page/impaired-driving)

### RIDE Program
- **Reducing Impaired Driving Everywhere (RIDE):** Police program using roadside sobriety checkpoints.
- Police may conduct random stops to check for impaired drivers; drivers are legally required to comply.
- No specific penalty information for RIDE found in fetched handbook sections — it is a screening/checkpoint program, not a separate offence tier.

### Ignition Interlock Device
- Vehicle will not start unless driver blows below **0.02 BAC** into the device.
- Random breath samples required while the vehicle is running.
- Required after impairment-level convictions (minimum 6 years) and can be required after serious warn-range repeat offences.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Ignition Interlock Program](https://www.ontario.ca/page/ignition-interlock-program)

## 12. G1 Licence Restrictions

- **Zero BAC:** Driver must have 0.00 blood alcohol at all times.
- **Accompanying driver:** A licensed driver with a minimum **Class G** licence and at least **4 years** of driving experience must sit in the front passenger seat.
  - The accompanying driver must also have a BAC under 0.05.
- **Prohibited highways:** Cannot drive on any **400-series highway** with a posted speed limit over 80 km/h.
- **Prohibited expressways:** Cannot drive on:
  - Queen Elizabeth Way (QEW)
  - Don Valley Parkway (DVP)
  - Gardiner Expressway
  - E.C. Row Expressway (Windsor)
  - Conestoga Parkway (Kitchener-Waterloo)
  - *Exception: G1 driver may use these roads if accompanied by a **licensed driving instructor**.*
- **Curfew:** Must not drive between **midnight and 5 a.m.**
- **Seatbelts:** Every occupant must have a working seatbelt; if anyone lacks one, G1 driver faces licence suspension.
- **Minimum G1 period:** 12 months before eligible for G2 road test.
  - Reduced to **8 months** if successfully completing an approved **Beginner Driver Education (BDE)** course.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Getting Your Driver's Licence](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/getting-your-drivers-licence)

## 13. G2 Licence Restrictions

- **Zero BAC:** Same zero-tolerance rule applies (driver must be 0.00 or, if 22+, under criminal 0.08 threshold — but zero BAC still applies if under 22 or holding G2 regardless of age).
  - *(All G1/G2/M1/M2 holders must maintain 0.00 BAC regardless of age.)*
- **No curfew** for G2 drivers **20 or older**.
- **Curfew for G2 drivers aged 19 and under:** Cannot drive midnight–5 a.m. with passengers unless accompanied by a fully licensed driver.
- **Passenger limits (G2 drivers aged 19 and under, midnight–5 a.m.):**
  - **First 6 months** of G2: Maximum **1 passenger** aged 19 or under
  - **After 6 months** (until full G or age 20): Maximum **3 passengers** aged 19 or under
  - *Exemptions:* Restrictions don't apply if a fully-licensed driver is in the front passenger seat, or if passengers are immediate family members.
- **Minimum G2 period:** 12 months before eligible for full G road test.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Getting Your Driver's Licence](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/getting-your-drivers-licence); [drivetest.ca](https://drivetest.ca/licences/drivers-licences-cars/)

## 14. Collisions — Reporting and Leaving the Scene

### When to Report to Police
- **Mandatory report required** when:
  - Any person is injured
  - Total vehicle/property damage exceeds **$2,000**
  - A criminal offence occurred (e.g., impaired driving, hit-and-run)
- **Under $2,000:** Police report not required, but drivers must still **exchange information** with all parties.
  - The $2,000 threshold has been in effect since September 1, 2015 (raised from $1,000).
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Dealing with Emergencies](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/dealing-emergencies)

### Information to Exchange
- Full name and address
- Vehicle registered owner's name and address
- Vehicle licence plate and permit number
- Liability insurance card details
- Names, addresses, phone numbers of witnesses

### Leaving the Scene
- **Every driver involved in a collision must remain at the scene or return to it immediately** and provide all possible assistance.
- Leaving the scene is a **7-demerit-point offence** under the Highway Traffic Act.
- Criminal Code "hit and run" charges may also apply.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Keeping Your Driver's Licence](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/keeping-your-drivers-licence)

### Non-Injury Collisions ("Steer It, Clear It")
- If vehicles are drivable and no injuries, move them off the travel lanes to a safe location before exchanging information.
- Document with photos if possible.

## 15. Emergency Vehicles and the Move-Over Law

### When an Emergency Vehicle Approaches with Lights/Siren
- Pull to the **right-hand curb** as near as practical.
- Signal in advance; brake gradually.
- Do not move until the emergency vehicle has passed.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Dealing with Particular Situations](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/dealing-particular-situations)

### Move-Over Law (Stopped Emergency or Tow Trucks)
- When approaching a **stopped emergency vehicle or tow truck** with flashing lights facing your direction of travel:
  - Reduce speed and proceed with caution.
  - If the road has **two or more lanes** in your direction: move into a **different lane** away from the stopped vehicle, if safe to do so.
- Applies to: police cars, fire trucks, ambulances, and tow trucks (amber flashing lights).
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Driving Near Emergency Vehicles](https://www.ontario.ca/page/driving-near-emergency-vehicles)

### Penalties for Failing to Move Over
- First offence: fine + **3 demerit points**
- Subsequent offences: increased fines + possible **jail up to 6 months**

### Volunteer Responders
- Volunteer firefighters and some volunteer medical responders may use **flashing green lights** in personal vehicles — yield to them as well.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Dealing with Particular Situations](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/dealing-particular-situations)

## 16. Roundabouts

- **Traffic direction:** Always travel **counter-clockwise** and keep to the right of the central island.
- **Yield rule:** Yield to vehicles **already in** the roundabout (circulating traffic has right-of-way over entering traffic).
- **Entry:** Watch for vehicles to your left; yield or stop at the yield sign before entering.
- **Lane selection:**
  - **Left lane:** for turning left or going straight
  - **Right lane:** for turning right or going straight
  - Do not enter from the right lane if you intend to turn left.
- **While circulating:** Do not stop (except to avoid a collision); do not change lanes.
- **Exiting:** Signal your exit after passing the exit before yours. Watch for pedestrians at the crosswalk when exiting.
- **Missed exit:** Continue circulating until you reach your exit again — do not reverse or cut across.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Changing Directions](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/changing-directions)

## 17. Railway Crossings

- **Stop distance from nearest rail or gate:** At least **5 metres**.
- **When to stop:**
  - Signals are flashing
  - Gates or barriers are down, being lowered, or being raised
  - A stop sign is posted at the crossing
- **Wait to proceed:** Until signals stop flashing **and** (if present) gates have fully risen.
- **Illegal:** Driving around, under, or through a gate or barrier at any stage of its operation.
- **Never stop on tracks:** In heavy traffic, ensure there is enough room to clear the tracks completely before beginning to cross.
- **If stalled on tracks:**
  1. Exit the vehicle immediately
  2. Move at least **30 metres** away from the tracks (to avoid injury from debris if a train strikes the vehicle)
  3. Contact police
- **Train stopping distance:** Up to **2 kilometres** under full emergency braking — do not assume a train can stop for you.
- **Penalties for failing to stop at railway crossing:** $110 fine ($150 in community safety zones) + 3 demerit points.
  - Source: [ontario.ca — Driving Near Railway Crossings](https://www.ontario.ca/page/driving-near-railway-crossings)

## 18. HOV Lanes (High-Occupancy Vehicle Lanes)

### Eligibility
- Vehicles with **at least 2 occupants** (cars, minivans, pickup trucks, motorcycles)
- Buses (all occupancy levels)
- Emergency vehicles (police, fire, ambulance)
- **Green-plate** electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids (any occupancy)
- Single-occupant taxis and airport limousines (permitted)
- Commercial motor vehicles: must have 2+ occupants and be **under 6.5 metres** in length

### Operating Rules
- Open **24 hours a day, 7 days a week** (unless posted otherwise)
- Separated from other lanes by striped buffer zones — cannot cross the buffer zone to enter/exit except at designated points
- Improper use results in a ticket; **3 demerit points** for HOV lane misuse.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Freeway Driving](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/freeway-driving)

## 19. Winter Driving / Adverse Conditions

### Snow and Ice
- Snow may be hard-packed and slippery as ice, or soft and deep.
- **Black ice:** Forms first in shaded areas and on bridges; appears as dark, shiny pavement rather than the typical grey-white winter look. Reduce speed when suspected.
- Avoid: sudden steering, braking, or acceleration on slippery surfaces.
- **Do not use cruise control** in snow or inclement weather.

### Whiteouts
- Blowing snow can reduce visibility to near-zero.
- Pull safely off the road if visibility becomes hazardous.
- Delay trips when weather warnings are in effect.

### Hydroplaning
- Occurs when tires ride on top of water rather than gripping pavement (like water skis).
- Causes: excessive speed on wet roads, worn tire tread, standing water.
- Prevention: reduce speed on wet roads; maintain adequate tire tread.

### Skid Recovery
- Ease off the accelerator and/or brakes.
- Continue steering in the direction you want to go.
- On extremely slippery surfaces, shifting to neutral may help.
- Avoid overcorrecting (oversteering).

### Fog
- Use **low-beam headlights** (high beams reflect off moisture and reduce visibility).
- Increase following distance.
- Use road edge markings as a guide rather than center lines.

### Following Distance in Bad Weather
- Increase beyond the 2-second standard; use at least 3 seconds as a baseline in adverse conditions.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Driving at Night and in Bad Weather](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/driving-night-and-bad-weather)

## 20. Sharing the Road — Cyclists

- **Minimum passing distance:** When overtaking a cyclist, drivers must maintain at least **1 metre** between the vehicle and the cyclist ("where practical").
- Cyclists may ride in the centre or left portion of a lane (e.g., to avoid hazards, parked car doors, railway tracks, or when preparing for a left turn).
- Cyclists may occupy a full lane and must be treated as any other vehicle.
- **Penalty for failing to maintain 1 m:** Fine + **2 demerit points**
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Sharing the Road with Other Road Users](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/sharing-road-other-road-users)

## 21. Sharing the Road — Motorcycles

- Motorcycles occupy a **full lane** — treat them as you would any other vehicle.
- Motorcycles are harder to see due to their small size; check mirrors and blind spots carefully.
- Motorcycle turn signals may not self-cancel; be cautious if a signal is on but the rider does not appear to be turning.
- Maintain full following distance (2-second rule) behind motorcycles.
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Sharing the Road with Other Road Users](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/sharing-road-other-road-users)

## 22. Sharing the Road — Large Trucks (Blind Spots)

Large commercial trucks have significant blind spots where the truck driver cannot see you:
- **Front:** Directly in front (close range)
- **Rear:** Directly behind the trailer (the most dangerous zone — if you can't see the truck's side mirrors, the driver can't see you)
- **Both sides:** Wide blind spots alongside and behind the cab

**Other hazards near large trucks:**
- Require much longer stopping distances than passenger vehicles
- Make **wide right turns** — may swing left before turning right; never cut between a turning truck and the curb
- Can roll backward momentarily when releasing brakes (when stopped on a hill)
- Create spray and reduced visibility in wet weather
- Generate air turbulence when passing at highway speeds
  - Source: [MTO Driver's Handbook — Sharing the Road with Other Road Users](https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook/sharing-road-other-road-users)

## 23. General Driving Knowledge

### Defensive Driving Principles
1. **Visibility:** Keep eyes constantly moving; scan ahead and to the sides; check mirrors every ~5 seconds.
2. **Space management:** Maintain a cushion of space ahead, behind, and to both sides.
3. **Communication:** Signal all intentions (slowing, stopping, turning, lane changes).

### Freeway Driving
- Scan 15–20 seconds ahead on highways.
- Keep right; use left lanes for passing only.
- Match speed in acceleration lanes before merging.
- Do not cut off vehicles when merging.

### Emergency Procedures
- **Brake failure:** Pump brake pedal; apply parking brake gently while holding the release button.
- **Stuck accelerator:** Lift with foot; shift to neutral; activate hazard lights.
- **Blown tire:** Reduce speed gradually; steer firmly; avoid heavy braking.
- **Wheels off pavement edge:** Maintain grip; avoid abrupt braking; steer gradually back.
- **Headlight failure:** Activate hazards; pull off road safely.
- **Freeway breakdown:** Pull to shoulder immediately; stay inside (locked) if safe; call for help.

### Road Signs (relevant to rules)
- **Regulatory signs:** Typically white/black or white/red — must be obeyed (stop, yield, speed limits).
- **Warning signs:** Yellow diamond — alert to hazards ahead.
- **Information signs:** Green/blue — directions, services.
- **Construction zones:** Orange background — temporary changes; fines doubled with workers present.
- **Community safety zones:** Posted with signage; standard fines doubled for traffic offences.
