the actual content ✨

the stuff they'll ask you

Tap a tab. Tap a flashcard to flip. The numbers in red are the ones the test loves the most — those are the "memorize verbatim" tier.

speed limits

The default trio

If there's no sign posted, Ontario assumes:

🏙 50 km/h in cities, towns, villages

🌾 80 km/h in rural areas

🛣 100 km/h on most 400-series highways (always obey posted)

following distance

The 2-second rule

Pick a fixed point (sign, pole). When the car ahead passes it, count "one-Mississippi, two-Mississippi". If you reach the point before two — too close.

In bad weather: 3+ seconds. In snow: 4+. Common sense, kinda.

signalling

Distance ≠ vibes

Signal at least 30m before a turn in a 50 km/h zone.

Signal at least 150m before on a highway.

Also signal before slowing, stopping, lane changing, leaving a parked spot.

4-way stop

Who goes first?

1. First to fully stop goes first.

2. Tie? Yield to the right.

3. Turning left? Yield to anyone going straight.

Test loves this. Memorize it.

roundabouts

Yield LEFT, not right

This trips everyone up. In a roundabout, you yield to traffic already inside — which comes from your left.

Travel counter-clockwise. Don't change lanes inside. Signal your exit.

Missed your exit? Just go around again. Don't reverse. Ever.

school bus

The Stop That Saves Lives™

🟡 Amber flashing = bus is slowing → prepare to stop

🔴 Red flashing + stop arm out = full stop, both directions on undivided roads

Stop at least 20m back. Wait until the lights stop or it moves.

Pass it = $400–$2,000 fine + 6 demerits. Don't.

pedestrian crossover (PXO)

Wait til they're ALL the way across

Not just past your lane. Across the entire road, on the sidewalk. Then go.

Bonus rule: never pass another vehicle within 30m of a PXO. Even if there's no pedestrian.

emergency vehicles

4-step pull-over

1. Slow down
2. Signal
3. Pull right (clear of intersections)
4. Stop until they pass

Stopped emergency or tow truck on the shoulder? Slow down + move one lane over if safe. (Move-Over Law.)

railway crossings

If gates drop, you stop

Stop at least 5 metres from the nearest rail when:

· Lights flashing
· Gates lowering, lowered, or rising
· Stop sign at the crossing
· Train approaching

Stalled on tracks? Get out. Walk 30m+ away. Call 911.

Tested often: school buses ALWAYS stop at rail crossings. Train or no train.

HOV lanes

Min 2 humans (or a green plate)

HOV = High-Occupancy Vehicle. You need at least 2 people in the car to use it.

Exception: green-plate EVs and plug-in hybrids can use it solo. Buses + emergency vehicles always allowed.

Open 24/7 unless posted. Cross only at the dashed entry/exit gaps. Misuse = 3 demerits.

skids

Steer where you wanna go

Car slides? Don't slam the brakes. Ease off the gas. Steer in the direction you actually want to travel.

Common wrong answer: "steer opposite the skid". Nope. Steer with your destination.

hydroplaning

Water-skiing but make it scary

Tires lose grip on standing water. Don't brake hard, don't accelerate. Ease off the gas, hold the wheel straight, wait for tires to regain contact.

night driving

Headlights = 150m of vision

Use headlights between sunset & sunrise, and any time visibility is under 150m (fog, heavy rain, snow).

Foggy? Low beams only. High beams reflect off moisture and blind you.

mirror checks

Every 5 seconds

Glance at mirrors every ~5 seconds. Scan 15–20 seconds ahead on highways. Eyes constantly moving — don't fixate.

parking distances

The metre trio

frommin distance
Fire hydrant3 m
Uncontrolled intersection9 m
Traffic light intersection15 m

3-9-15. Just memorize the rhythm.

parking on a hill

Wheel-turn cheat

🔼 Uphill with curb: turn wheels left (away from curb)

🔽 Downhill with curb: turn wheels right (toward curb)

🛤 Hill no curb: turn wheels right (toward shoulder), either direction

Logic: if brakes fail, car rolls away from traffic.

tires

Tread depth ≥ 1.5 mm

Below that, replace. Or when the wear indicators touch the road. Winter tires aren't legally required in Ontario (but most insurers give a discount).

cyclists

Give 'em a metre

Pass cyclists with at least 1 metre of space. Less than that = fine + 2 demerits.

Cyclists can take the full lane — treat them like a car.

trucks

If you can't see their mirrors…

…the trucker can't see you. Big trucks have huge blind spots in front, directly behind, and along both sides of the cab.

They make wide right turns — never squeeze between a turning truck and the curb.

car seat stages

The kid-seat ladder

Booster exit = ANY one of the three thresholds, not all three.

Never put a rear-facing seat in front of an active airbag.

G1 rule #1

Zero. Alcohol.

Your BAC must be 0.00. Always. No "one drink". No "I had a sip". Zero.

Same applies to G2, M1, M2, and anyone 21 or under.

supervisor

You can never drive alone

Front passenger seat must have a fully licensed driver (Class G+) with at least 4 years of driving experience.

That person also has to be under 0.05 BAC. Don't let drunk uncle "supervise".

curfew

Bed by midnight 🌙

No driving between midnight and 5 a.m. Any day. Any reason. Even with a supervisor.

no big highways

400-series? not yet

Cannot drive on any 400-series highway, the QEW, DVP, Gardiner, E.C. Row, or Conestoga.

Exception: you're with a licensed driving instructor.

passengers

One belt per body

Every passenger must have a working seatbelt. If anyone in the car is unbelted, you get a min 30-day suspension. No sharing belts. No seven cousins in the back.

timeline

How long until G2?

Standard wait: 12 months from your G1 date.

With approved Beginner Driver Education (BDE) course: 8 months. Saves you 4.

G2 → full G is another 12 months (no BDE shortcut there).

two test favourites

Two random rules they LOVE

1. Never lend your licence. To anyone. Ever. It's illegal full-stop. (MTO sample-test classic.)

2. If a police officer is directing traffic and the signal disagrees with them — obey the cop. Officers override every sign and light.

Demerit points are the test's favourite trick category. Memorize the worst ones — those are the ones the test will throw at you.

7 demerits 💀

Leaving the scene

Leaving the scene of a collision = 7 demerits (max single-offence). Failing to stop for police = same.

6 demerits

Big-girl offences

· Careless driving
· Racing / street racing
· Speeding 50+ over (on 80+ roads)
· Speeding 40+ over (on roads under 80)
· Failing to stop for school bus

4 demerits

Mid offences

· Speeding 30–49 km over
· Following too closely
· Failing to yield at PXO

3 demerits

The basics

· Speeding 16–29 km over
· Improper turn / failing to signal
· Disobeying stop sign
· HOV misuse
· Failing to move over for emergency

your suspension threshold

9 points = bye 👋

Novice (G1, G2, M1, M2) drivers get a 60-day suspension at 9 points.

Fully licensed: 30-day suspension at 15 points. Y'all have buffer. We don't.

stunt driving

The career-ender

Triggered by:

· 40+ over on roads under 80
· 50+ over on roads 80+
· 150 km/h anywhere

Roadside: 30-day suspension + 14-day vehicle impound. Court: $2k–$10k fine, 1-yr to lifetime ban.

distracted driving

Phone in hand = no.

For novices (you, hi):

1st offence: 30-day suspension

2nd: 90-day suspension

3rd: licence cancelled + kicked out of GLS

Hands-free is fine. Voice GPS is fine. Holding the phone = not fine.

the $2,000 collision rule

When to call police

Required if: anyone's injured, total damage >$2,000, or a crime happened (impaired, hit-and-run).

Under $2k & no injuries? Just exchange info. Take photos.

BAC for everyone else

Full-G drinking limits (FYI tier)

You're zero, but the test sometimes asks about full-G drivers:

· 0.05–0.079 = "warn range" → roadside 3-day suspension + $250

· 0.08+ = Criminal Code offence → 90-day roadside + 7-day impound

G1/G2/M1/M2/under-22 = always 0.00. No warn range for us.

visual recognition core 👁

signs are like flashcards the test pretends are questions

The signs part is mostly "what does this picture mean?". Good news: if you know the shape and colour, you can usually guess right — even on a sign you've never seen. Learn these 9 shapes and you're 80% there.

the shapes 📐

Stop
Red octagon. Full stop — wheels not moving. Look both ways, then go if clear.
Yield
Upside-down triangle. "Yield" = let other cars go first. Slow down, wait, then go.
Warning (yellow ◇)
Yellow diamond = something ahead (curve, hill, bump). Slow down, eyes up.
Construction (orange ◇)
Same diamond but ORANGE = workers on the road right now. Slow way down — tickets cost 2× here.
School zone
House-shape, bright yellow-green = kids around. Slow way down.
Speed limit
White box with a number = the fastest you're allowed. Going over = ticket.
Destination (green)
Green sign = which way to a place + how far. Just info, no rule.
Service (blue)
Blue sign = stuff you can stop for: gas, food, hospital, hotel.
Railway (advance)
Yellow circle with X = train tracks ahead. Slow down, look + listen for a train.
Crossbuck
Big white X = you're AT the tracks. Stop if a train is coming or the lights flash.
Do Not Enter
Don't drive in. Cars are coming the OTHER way — you'd hit them head-on.
One Way
Cars go one direction only — the way the arrow points.
Roundabout ahead
Circle ahead. Slow down, look LEFT — cars already in the circle go first. Wait for a gap, then go.
Slippery when wet
Road gets slippery when it rains. Slow down.
Deer crossing
Deer cross here. Slow down at sunrise/sunset — they jump out fast.
No U-Turn
No U-turn here. Don't flip around to go the other way.
Slow-Moving Vehicle
Orange triangle on the back of a farm truck or tractor. Means it's going under 40 km/h — pass carefully.
Pavement-line rule: Yellow separates traffic going opposite directions. White separates traffic going the same direction. That single rule unlocks like 5 quiz questions.

painted-on-the-road extras 🛣

continuity lines

Wider, closer-spaced white dashes

Means your lane is ending or exiting. Change lanes if you want to keep going straight. They're a warning, not an invitation.

two-way left turn lane

The shared middle lane

Centre lane bordered by double yellow with a broken yellow inside, plus left-turn arrows in both directions. Either side uses it to stage a left turn — never to drive in.

bike box

Don't sit in the painted box

Box with a bike symbol at the intersection = bike waiting area. Cars stop behind it. Bikes get to slot in front for the green.

HOV diamond + arrows

Pavement tells you the rules too

White ◇ painted on the road = HOV lane. White arrows in a lane = you may only go that direction. Crosswalk = two parallel white lines; PXO adds a giant X painted in each lane.

show up prepared, leave with a licence

step 1 · before

Bring this stuff

· Original ID with full legal name + DOB + signature (passport, citizenship card, PR card, birth certificate)

· Glasses/contacts if you wear them — eye exam first

· Debit or credit card

· Be 16+

step 2 · arrive

Walk in (no booking needed)

Most DriveTest centres take walk-ins. Show up during business hours, do the eye test, pay $159.75, write the test on a touchscreen.

Check your local centre's hours though, some are weird about Mondays.

step 3 · the test

Pace yourself

It's untimed. Read every question fully. All 4 answers will sound plausible — pick the most specific or most cautious.

If torn between two: the safer/more defensive option is usually right.

step 4 · after

You pass — now what?

G1 issued same day. Valid 5 years. Then:

· 12 months until G2 road test (or 8 with BDE course)

· You can start driving with a supervisor immediately

Failed? Pay ~$16 and retry. No mandatory wait.

where ppl mess up 🪤

the trap questions

These are the ones the test asks the same way every time, and that everyone gets wrong on first try. Tap to expand.

Roundabout: which way do I yield?

Instinct says "yield right" because that's how 4-way stops work. Wrong. In a roundabout you yield to traffic already inside — and since traffic circulates counter-clockwise and you enter from the right, that's traffic coming from your LEFT.

✓ Yield to vehicles inside the roundabout (from your left)
School bus: amber vs red flashing — what's the difference?

Amber (yellow) flashing = bus is slowing to stop. Slow down + prepare to stop, but you don't legally have to stop yet.

Red flashing + stop arm out = bus stopped, kids loading/unloading. Full stop required, both directions on undivided roads.

✓ Amber = prepare. Red = STOP.
Skid recovery: which way do I steer?

Wrong-answer trap: "steer opposite the skid". Nope.

The MTO answer: steer in the direction you want to travel. If your back end slides right, you want to keep going forward — so steer right (which happens to be with the slide). Don't slam brakes. Ease off the gas.

✓ Steer where you want to go. Easy on the brakes.
Pedestrian crossover vs. crosswalk — when do I drive again?

At a crossover (PXO, mid-block w/ overhead lights): you wait until the pedestrian is completely across the road and on the sidewalk. Not just past your lane.

At a regular intersection crosswalk: yield to pedestrians in your path, but you can move once they've cleared your turn radius.

Bonus: never pass another car within 30 metres of a PXO.

✓ PXO = wait til they're ALL the way across, every time.
U-turn: what's the FIRST thing to check?

Common wrong answers: "is there enough room", "can I see oncoming". Plausible — but not the MTO's official #1.

The official answer: check traffic regulations. Is the U-turn even legal here? Sign? Intersection rules? If yes, then check sightlines + room.

✓ Legal first, physical possibility second.
4-way stop: 4 cars arrive at the exact same moment. Now what?

This is rare in the wild but the test loves it. The rule: yield to the right. Each driver yields to the vehicle on their right.

In a perfect deadlock, eye contact + slow waves work. The test isn't testing diplomacy though — pick "yield to the right".

✓ Yield right.
Solid + broken yellow line — can I pass?

If you're on the side with the broken line, yes you may pass when safe. If you're on the side with the solid line, no.

Always read YOUR side of the line, not the other side.

✓ Broken on your side = green light to pass.
Power outage at a traffic light — what do I do?

Don't just send it. Treat the dead intersection as an all-way stop. Stop fully, yield to vehicles already in or to your right, then proceed.

✓ Dark light = 4-way stop.
Foggy day — high beams or low beams?

Counter-intuitive: low beams. High beams reflect off the moisture droplets and create a wall of glare that reduces visibility further.

✓ Low beams in fog. Always.
I see flashing red lights AT a railway crossing. Train isn't here yet. Can I cross?

No. Flashing red = stop. Wait until lights stop flashing AND (if there's a gate) it's fully raised. A train is coming or already passing — you cannot judge train speed/distance accurately.

✓ Lights flashing = full stop. Wait it out.

the actual tl;dr cheat sheet

If you read literally nothing else, read this. These are the highest-frequency facts on the actual test.

📏 Numbers to memorize

  • Default urban speed: 50 km/h
  • Default rural speed: 80 km/h
  • Following: 2 sec normal, 3+ sec rain, 4+ sec snow/ice
  • Signal before turn: 30 m in city, 150 m on hwy
  • Stop behind school bus: 20 m
  • Stop from rail: 5 m
  • Park from hydrant / intersection / light: 3 / 9 / 15 m
  • Pass cyclist: 1 m minimum
  • Tire tread minimum: 1.5 mm
  • Headlight visibility: 150 m
  • HOV lane: 2+ occupants (or green-plate EV)
  • Lending your licence: never. illegal.

🚦 Light cheat sheet

  • Green = go, yield to peds
  • Yellow = stop if safe
  • Red = stop (right turn ok after stop)
  • Flashing green = advance left, you own it
  • Flashing red = stop sign
  • Flashing yellow = caution
  • Power out = 4-way stop
  • Red X overhead = lane closed

🛑 Right-of-way

  • 4-way stop tie: yield right
  • Uncontrolled intersection: yield right
  • Roundabout: yield left (inside traffic)
  • Left turn: yield to oncoming + peds
  • Entering from driveway: yield to all
  • Pedestrians at PXO: wait til fully across
  • Police > traffic light. Always.

🆔 G1 rules

  • BAC: 0.00 always
  • Supervisor: G+ licence, 4+ years, BAC < 0.05
  • No driving midnight–5 a.m.
  • No 400-series / QEW / Gardiner / DVP / E.C. Row / Conestoga
  • One belt per body
  • Wait 12 mo for G2 (or 8 with BDE)

🪧 Sign quick-decode

  • Octagon → STOP
  • Triangle (point down) → YIELD
  • Yellow ◇ → permanent warning
  • Orange ◇ → construction
  • Pentagon → school zone
  • Yellow circle → railway ahead
  • X-buck → at the railway
  • Green = info · Blue = services · Brown = parks

💸 Big-deal demerits

  • Leaving scene: 7
  • School bus pass: 6 + $400–$2,000
  • Careless / racing: 6
  • Speeding 50+: 6
  • Following too close: 4
  • Failing to yield PXO: 4
  • Novice suspension threshold: 9 pts

🌧 Bad weather moves

  • Fog → low beams, never high
  • Snow → at least 4 sec following
  • Hydroplane → ease off gas, steer straight
  • Skid → steer where you want to go
  • No cruise control in snow / heavy rain
  • Black ice = invisible. Bridges first.
